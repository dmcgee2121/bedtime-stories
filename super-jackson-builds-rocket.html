🚀 Super Jackson Builds a Rocket

Part 2: Mission to the Moon

One cloudy afternoon, Super Jackson was bored. No mysteries to solve. No shoes to find. Just a quiet house and a very silly dad.

“Dad,” Jackson said, “I want to go on an adventure. A BIG one!”

Dad grinned. “How big are we talking? Like… backyard big? Or outer space big?”

Jackson’s eyes lit up. “Outer space! Let’s build a rocket!”

So they gathered supplies:

  • One giant cardboard box
  • Two paper towel tubes (for boosters!)
  • Lots of shiny foil
  • And stickers. SO many stickers.

They taped and wrapped and decorated until the rocket was ready. It had buttons made from bottle caps and a steering wheel made from a frisbee.

“Ready for launch?” Dad asked.

Jackson climbed inside. “Ready, Captain Silly!”

“3… 2… 1… BLAST OFF!”

The rocket shook (because Dad was pushing it), and zoomed through the living room, past the couch nebula and the lamp asteroid belt.

“We’re approaching the moon!” Jackson shouted.

They landed with a soft thud (on a pillow). Jackson stepped out and planted a flag made from a sock and a chopstick.

“We did it!” he said. “We’re the first pizza-loving astronauts on the moon!”

Dad handed him a slice. “Space explorers need snacks.”

They munched, they giggled, and they looked up at the stars painted on the ceiling.

“Next time,” Jackson said, “we should visit Mars.”

Dad nodded. “But only if they have pepperoni.”

And with that, Super Jackson’s rocket adventure came to a cheesy, giggly end.

The End (until next mission).
